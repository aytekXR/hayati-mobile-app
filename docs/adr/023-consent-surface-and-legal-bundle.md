- **Related:** ADR-017 (the per-device coach-disclaimer consent precedent), ADR-018 (device-privacy honest bounds), ADR-019 (data rights: export wire, cascade, the server-owned-users-field rules invariant), ADR-020 (store identity, the over-claim blacklist, the empty-URL ratchet), ADR-021 (release lane), ADR-022 (pre-frame bootstrap sentinel)

## Context

`mvp.md` item 12 — *"Legal: privacy policy TR/AR/EN, KVKK/PDPL self-serve delete + export, terms, AI disclaimer"* — is the last MVP unit with no founder-blocked half. The delete + export halves shipped in M6.2 (ADR-019) and the in-app AI disclaimer shipped in M5.2 (ADR-017 D4). What remains, and what `architecture.md` §8 records honestly as **unbuilt**, is: consent screens per locale, the privacy policy and terms texts, and the DPA inventory. M6.3 additionally left the store's privacy/support URL fields deliberately EMPTY behind the `--allow-empty-urls` lint ratchet (ADR-020) — the policy text this ADR produces is the missing content for exactly that URL, though hosting stays founder-gated (operator item 8(c)).

This ADR was designed against three evidence briefs researched 2026-07-13 (KVKK incl. the 2024 Law-7499 amendments and the July-2024 transfer regulation; Saudi PDPL incl. the Sept-2024 full-enforcement posture and the still-unenacted 2025 draft amendments; Apple App Review Guidelines rev. June 2026). The briefs are evidence, **not legal advice**; where a reading is load-bearing and genuinely ambiguous, this ADR records both readings, implements the conservative one, and flags the founder's-lawyer question in `operator-expected.md` (the resume-prompt stopping condition — we do not play counsel).

**The shipped reality the legal surface must describe** (over-claiming is the failure mode — S020's guarantee-vs-mechanism class applied to legal text):

- Data collected: auth identifiers (name/email/photo from Apple/Google, phone number for OTP), profile (`status`, `contentLanguage`, `register`), free-text solo reflections and couple answers (≤2000 chars), couple metadata (pairing, timezone, streak), coach usage **counters** (never coach text), subscription entitlement mirror, invite records. All in Firestore **eur3 (EU multi-region)**.
- The coach is **ephemeral**: zero conversation content persisted server- or app-side (ADR-016/017); crisis detection runs transiently and no crisis flag is ever stored against a user (ADR-016 log discipline: crisis-path lines drop even `coupleId`).
- **No analytics SDK exists** (no Mixpanel, no Firebase Analytics — mvp item 11 is unbuilt); Crashlytics is prod-only and content-free by sentinel-pinned rule. **No marketing/push delivery exists** (the APNs device half is operator item 4; `fcmTokens` is never written).
- Export (own data only, in-app, no email) and hard delete (whole-thread terminal, no push to the partner, billing survives) shipped in M6.2 with their honest bounds already in app copy.

### The legal-basis map (the analytical core)

| Processing | Basis (KVKK) | Basis (PDPL, at GCC phase) | Consent needed? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Auth identifiers, profile, pairing, streaks, entitlements — the service the user signs up for | Contract necessity (Art 5/2-c) | Contract/agreement implementation | **No** — and wrapping it in consent would be the KVKK "misleading consent" error (Icerik/5412) |
| Free-text reflections + couple answers + AI-coach messages + transient crisis detection | **Special-category, conservative reading** → explicit consent (Art 6, as amended 2024) | Sensitive data (health-inference via crisis detection; belief content in free text) → explicit consent, legitimate-interest unavailable | **Yes — exactly one explicit, separate, unbundled consent** |
| Analytics / profiling | açık rıza required (KVKK cookie/analytics decisions) | consent | Not collected — **feature does not exist**; consent arrives with the feature |
| Marketing / promotional push (İYS/ETK) | opt-in via İYS | consent | Not collected — **no push delivery exists**; recorded as a forward obligation on item 4's APNs slice |

**Load-bearing ambiguity 1 — special-category classification.** *Permissive reading:* relationship reflections are user-authored ordinary content; a keyword crisis-detector is a safety feature, not health processing. *Conservative reading (implemented):* free-text intimacy Q&A predictably contains sexual-life/health content, and the crisis detector derives a mental-health signal from text — together special-category processing under KVKK Art 6 / PDPL sensitive data, for which the only realistic basis is explicit consent. → **Lawyer question A** (recorded in operator-expected).

**Load-bearing ambiguity 2 — consent as a condition of service.** KVKK consent must be freely given and cannot be extorted as a service condition (Icerik/5412); PDPL's anti-bundling rule carves out processing "specifically related" to the service. *Permissive reading:* the reflective content IS the product, so requiring this consent to use the reflective features is not conditioning — it is the PDPL carve-out and the KVKK rule's intended scope (the vice is tying *unrelated* processing). *Conservative reading (implemented):* same gate, but the consent copy states plainly WHY it is required (the processing is the service itself), declining leaves sign-out and account deletion available and un-nagged, and nothing else (no analytics, no marketing) rides the same consent. → **Lawyer question B**.

## Decision 1 — Exactly one consent, not a toggle farm; the notice is not a consent

The consent surface collects **one** explicit consent: processing of the user's relationship reflections, shared answers, and coach messages (the special-category-conservative bundle). There are **no per-purpose toggles** in v1 because no optional processing exists to toggle — analytics and marketing consents ship *with those features*, as their own unbundled opt-ins, when the features are built. The KVKK aydınlatma (information notice) is presented as a **notice, never a checkbox** — the Tebliğ requires notice and consent to be separate operations, and the KVKK's own guidance says consenting to what a contract basis already covers *misleads the data subject*.

**Rejected: a first-run consent wall covering everything.** Bundled "I accept everything" consent is legally invalid (battaniye rıza) and would poison the one consent we actually need.
**Rejected: per-purpose toggle farm now.** Toggles for processing that does not exist is consent theatre; every toggle would be a false claim about a live capability (the S020 class).
**Rejected: no consent at all (pure contract basis).** Defensible under the permissive reading, but it bets the launch on the aggressive side of both ambiguities; the Kurul is most aggressive exactly on special-category mishandling.

## Decision 2 — Notice placement: pre-auth access from the sign-in screen; documents render in-app

The aydınlatma must precede collection, and collection starts at sign-in (auth identifiers). The sign-in screen (`_SignedOutView`) gains a **legal footer**: one short line plus two text links — Privacy Notice and Terms — opening the in-app document screens (Decision 5). No checkbox, no acknowledgement, no blocking step pre-auth: the duty is availability-before-collection, and a pre-auth ack would have nowhere durable to live anyway. The pre-auth `PartnerPreviewScreen` collects nothing of the visitor and needs no gate.

Apple 5.1.1(i) requires the privacy policy *"within the app in an easily accessible manner"* — the in-app documents satisfy this **without waiting for the founder's domain** (no hosted URL exists; the store URL fields stay empty behind the ADR-020 ratchet, unchanged this session).

**Rejected: notice-as-first-run-interstitial (blocking splash).** Heavier than the duty requires, adds a pre-auth screen with no durable state, and pushes users through a legal wall before they can even see what the app is.

## Decision 3 — The consent gate: a per-uid `OnboardingGate` branch, after profile capture, before every home

A new gate-child branch in `OnboardingGate`, evaluated **immediately after the `value == null → ProfileCaptureScreen` branch and before every other branch** (paired home, coupleEnded notice, pending invite, solo home):

```
value != null && !hasCurrentConsent(value)  →  ConsentGateScreen(uid)
```

where `hasCurrentConsent` = `profile.consent != null && profile.consent.version >= currentLegalVersion`. Gate children are rendered, never pushed (uncloseable by back — the `CoupleEndedNoticeScreen` mold). `PrivacyGuard` already sits above it.

- **After profile capture, deliberately.** Profile fields (`status`, `contentLanguage`, `register`) are ordinary data on the contract basis and are needed to render the gate in the right language and register; the special-category consent gates the *reflective content*, which begins at the homes. This also means the `recordConsent` callable can use the fail-closed `.update()` mold against an existing `users/{uid}` doc (Decision 4). *Rejected: gate before profile capture* — it would force a create-or-merge write path (weaker than `.update()`'s native profile-missing failure) and would gate ordinary-basis collection behind a consent that isn't about it.
- **Before the `coupleEnded` notice, deliberately:** consent is a processing precondition; notices are content. B returning after an app update sees consent once, then the notice.
- **The screen:** summary paragraphs (what is processed, why, where — EU hosting named as a cross-border transfer, the processors, the rights, the withdrawal path), links to both full documents, an 18+ attestation line directly above the CTA ("By continuing you confirm you are at least 18"), **one affirmative `FilledButton`** ("I consent and continue" — a deliberate action, never a pre-ticked box), and a secondary **sign-out** `TextButton` so a user who declines is not trapped. Busy/error handling follows the settings-row pattern (busy flag, `SettingsErrorLine`-style resolver, honest failure copy, retry).
- **No optimistic local grant:** the gate flips only when the server-confirmed field arrives on the already-streaming `profileStreamProvider` — offline acceptance shows the error line and retries; a device-local "consented" that the server never saw would be an unprovable consent.
- **Version bump ⇒ re-gate:** `currentLegalVersion` is a Dart const; a future material policy revision bumps it (plus the server's accepted set and the document front-matter, same diff — see Decision 4's honest costs) and every user re-consents on next open.
- **Migration story (recorded, not silent):** existing sessions — the founder couple — have no `consent` field; on first open after this ships they see the consent screen **once**, one tap, then their session continues exactly as before. Nothing else about their state is touched.
- **Bootstrap untouched:** the gate reads the streamed profile; no new pre-frame await, no `entrypoint_sentinel_test` edit, no new `LocalFlagStore` key.

## Decision 4 — Consent state is server-owned: `users/{uid}.consent` via a `recordConsent` callable

`users/{uid}.consent = { version: <int>, acceptedAt: <server Timestamp> }`, written **only** by a new `recordConsent` callable (admin SDK), following the `updateNotificationPrivacy` mold byte-for-byte: pure validator in `data-rights-core.ts` (accepts `{version}` from the **known set `[1]` only** — junk and future versions are `invalid-argument`; the server never trusts a client-claimed version), `.update()` service (missing profile fails natively → `failed-precondition {reason:'profile-missing'}`), onCall shell with the static-error discipline and `logDataRightsEvent` (op/outcome/latency only — never uid, never version-of-a-named-user).

- **Why server-side, not the ADR-017 device-local precedent:** KVKK puts the proof burden (ispat yükü) on the controller — consent must be demonstrable with version + timestamp, must survive reinstall, and is itself personal data (export + erasure). A `LocalFlagStore` flag proves nothing, dies with prefs, and is invisible to export. The coach ack stays device-local because it is a UX acknowledgement, not a legal consent — the distinction is now written down.
- **The M6.2 rules invariant applies in full** (ADR-019 D6: *every server-owned users field needs an update freeze AND a create forbid*): the users create rule gains `!('consent' in request.resource.data)`, the update rule gains `request.resource.data.get('consent', null) == resource.data.get('consent', null)`, and both clauses get the four-part mutant treatment (create-forbid mutant, freeze mutant, positive control, delete-deny already covered).
- **A field, deliberately not a subcollection.** `users/{uid}/consents/*` would need a new cascade sweep step (the M6.2 own-subtree sweep enumerates only `soloAnswers` — an unswept subcollection is orphaned residue), a new rules block against the deny-all catch-all, and an append-only history the data-minimization posture doesn't want. The field rides the existing cascade's `deleteRef(selfRef)` untouched. **Honest cost:** only the *latest* acceptance is retained — re-consent to v2 overwrites the v1 record. The proof burden concerns the basis of *current* processing, which the latest record carries; recorded as the accepted trade-off.
- **Withdrawal — as easy as giving (KVKK/PDPL both require it):** the same callable accepts `{withdraw: true}` and deletes the field (`FieldValue.delete()`, the `notificationPrivacy` clear mold). The gate then reappears on next evaluation. Withdrawal UX lives on the settings-reachable legal screen (Decision 5): consent status line ("Consented on <date>, version N"), a "Withdraw consent" action behind one confirm dialog whose copy is honest — withdrawal stops further reflective-feature use (the gate returns); it does **not** erase what exists (that is Delete account & data, one row up); billing is untouched by either.
- **Export lane:** `projectProfile` gains `consent: {version, acceptedAtMs}` **iff set** — the exact `notificationPrivacy` precedent. Additive optional field; **`formatVersion` stays 1** (the version exists so parsers know the shape; an optional addition breaks no parser). Recorded as the deliberate reading of ADR-019's frozen-wire rule; the review may overturn to a bump.
- **Client parse is junk-safe:** the app `Profile` model parses `consent` tolerantly (non-map, missing keys, non-int version ⇒ treated as absent ⇒ gate shows — fail closed).

## Decision 5 — In-app legal documents: authored in `docs/legal/`, byte-synced to bundled assets, rendered without new dependencies

- **Authoring home:** `docs/legal/privacy-policy.{tr,ar,en}.md` and `docs/legal/terms.{tr,ar,en}.md` (+ `docs/legal/README.md` carrying the version, the review-PENDING flags, and the bump procedure). The TR privacy policy is structured to double as the **aydınlatma metni** — it carries the Art-10 minimums (controller identity, purposes, recipient groups, collection method **and legal basis stated explicitly per purpose**, Art-11 rights) and the Apple 5.1.1(i) minimums (data/uses, third parties held to equal protection, retention/deletion, consent revocation) and the PDPL Art-12 content (design-ahead). One document per locale serving all three masters — a solo-founder app does not need parallel instruments.
- **Bundle sync:** the six documents are byte-copied to `app/assets/legal/` (new pubspec asset dir). Drift is pinned by an app test that reads both trees and asserts byte-equality (`../docs/legal/...` is reachable from `flutter test`'s `app/` cwd — the shipped-packs-off-disk precedent). The content-pack `--sync` tool precedent is deliberately NOT extended — six static files don't need a tool, they need a red CI on drift.
- **Rendering:** a minimal deterministic renderer (~40 lines): `#`/`##` lines → title/section styles, `- ` lines → bulleted rows, blank-line-separated paragraphs → body `Text`. Total function — any unrecognized line renders as body text; it can never throw. Inside the established `SingleChildScrollView → Column(Text…)` mold. **Rejected: `flutter_markdown`/`url_launcher`/WebView** — each would be the app's first such dependency, for six static documents; and a URL launcher has no URL to launch until the founder hosts one.
- **Surfaces:** `LegalScreen` (hub: two document tiles + consent status + withdraw action when signed-in) pushed via `showLegal(context, {uid})`; `LegalDocumentScreen` renders one document in the resolved locale (EN fallback). Reachable from: the sign-in footer (pre-auth, documents only — no consent status), the consent gate's "read the documents" links, a new Settings tile (between Export and Delete — the data-rights cluster), and a **paywall links row** (Terms + Privacy, Apple 3.1.2/Schedule-2 requires both tappable on the subscription surface). Links are `TextButton`s (the S019 `FilledButton` min-width gotcha).
- **ARB:** new `legal*` and `consent*` prefixes ×3 locales for all chrome and consent copy, guarded by a new `legal_arb_guard_test.dart` (tri-locale parity + placeholder shape; the coach digit-run rule deliberately not cloned — these documents legitimately contain no phone numbers anyway).

## Decision 6 — The draft texts: honest to the shipped reality, review-PENDING, in the respectful registers

AI-drafted, flagged `reviewedBy: PENDING` in `docs/legal/README.md`, and **binding on this session's prose** (the trap named in the resume-prompt: every sentence a lawyer would strike is a sentence the founder must be able to review against the code):

- **Registers:** TR-respectful (the ADR-020 D8 precedent — legal surfaces are read by the partner's world too), AR MSA-Gulf formal-warm, EN plain. Brand voice minus playfulness; never clinical, never boilerplate.
- **The honest-bounds vocabulary is reused, not paraphrased:** deletion is whole-thread terminal, actor-unattributed to the partner, sends no push, does not cancel App Store billing, cannot un-reveal what a partner already saw; export is strictly-your-own data, in-app, no email; the coach is ephemeral (nothing stored server- or app-side), is not therapy, and its crisis detection runs transiently with no stored flag; the lock and discreet icon carry their ADR-018 bounds (the label under the icon does not change; casual access is blocked, an identity-anchor holder is detectable-not-preventable); notifications: **no push is delivered today** — the policy describes notification *settings* honestly as applying to future delivery.
- **The AI section states the no-training guarantee as what it is:** a contractual requirement that will bind the LLM provider **when one is selected** (operator item 6) — today no provider exists and no coach text leaves the transient call. Over-claiming "your data is never used for training" as a live mechanism is the exact S020 gap.
- **Terms:** 18+ (the conservative age posture — KVKK discernment risk, PDPL guardian-consent avoidance, the couples framing), the couple-data reality (your partner reads what you share; deletion semantics by reference to the policy), subscription terms honest to ADR-014/015 (one purchase covers both, App Store billing, deletion ≠ cancellation), coach acceptable-use (not therapy/medical/legal advice; crisis redirect), governing law left as a bracketed founder/legal decision — **not invented**.
- **What the texts never claim** (the ADR-020 blacklist extended to legal): end-to-end encryption (Firestore default at-rest is named as what it is), "we cannot read your data", "all your data" for export, hiding/disguising the app, analytics that don't exist, push that doesn't deliver, or any retention window that isn't real (nothing auto-expires today except invite *validity* — 48h validity, records retained until deletion; stated honestly).
- **Custom Terms vs Apple's standard EULA:** the store defaults to Apple's standard EULA; our Terms govern the service relationship in-app. Whether to file the custom Terms as the store EULA at submission is a founder/legal call recorded in operator-expected, not made here.

## Decision 8 — The app-level privacy manifest (`PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy`)

The Runner target gains a `PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy` declaring (a) the app's collected-data types mirroring the future App Privacy label answers (contact info, user ID, user content, purchase history, crash data — all app-functionality, none tracking; `NSPrivacyTracking=false`) and (b) the `NSPrivacyAccessedAPICategoryUserDefaults` required-reason entry (`CA92.1` — `shared_preferences`). Wired into the Resources build phase by hand-authored pbxproj edit (the S022 `InfoPlist.strings` precedent) and asserted by a new `ios-build-smoke` check that the built `Runner.app` **contains** the manifest (the S020 silent-green class — an unwired plist fails invisibly). Scope-justified: it is a privacy-compliance artifact of exactly this legal bundle and a live submission-rejection cause; SDK-shipped manifests cover the SDKs, not the app. Honest bound: full validation (Xcode privacy report, App Store Connect ingestion) is Mac-era and rides item 4's checklist.

## Decision 9 — Fail-direction rules

| Situation | Direction |
|---|---|
| `recordConsent` body malformed / version not in the accepted set | `invalid-argument`, nothing written |
| `recordConsent` with no `users/{uid}` doc | native NOT_FOUND → `failed-precondition {profile-missing}`, nothing written |
| Client writes/mints `users.consent` directly | rules deny (create-forbid + update-freeze, mutant-pinned) |
| Profile stream errors at the gate | existing `_GateErrorView` retry — **no consent assumed, no bypass** |
| `consent` field junk-shaped on read | parsed as absent → gate shows (fail closed) |
| Accept while offline / callable fails | error line + retry on the gate; **never** an optimistic local grant |
| Withdrawal | field deleted server-side → gate reappears; no partial state |
| Legal asset missing at runtime | drift test makes it unbuildable in CI; renderer itself is total and never throws |
| Version bump without server-set/docs update | server rejects the client's new version (`invalid-argument`) — loud, not silent; bump procedure in `docs/legal/README.md` |

## Consequences

**Positive:** mvp item 12's buildable half closes — the last unblocked MVP unit; consent is provable (server-stamped version + timestamp), exportable, erasable, and withdrawable; Apple 5.1.1(i)/3.1.2 in-app requirements are met without waiting on the founder's domain; the store's missing privacy-policy URL now has content waiting to be hosted; the DPA inventory turns §8's claims into a checkable register; the app-level privacy manifest closes a known submission-rejection cause.

**Negative / accepted trade-offs:** one more gate screen between a new user and the product (mitigated: one tap, shown once per version); the founder couple re-consents once on update (recorded migration story); only the latest consent record is retained (minimization over audit-trail — the latest record carries the current basis); `currentLegalVersion` lives as two constants (app + functions) coordinated by procedure, not by a cross-tree test (a version drift fails loud at the callable, not silent); the legal drafts are AI-drafted and worthless as legal instruments **until the founder's lawyer and native reviewers pass them** — they ship review-PENDING and the app presents them as its real documents in the meantime (the alternative — shipping nothing — leaves the app collecting special-category data with no notice at all, which is strictly worse); the conservative special-category posture may be more consent than the law demands (lawyer question A can relax it in a future version; the reverse migration — adding a missing consent later — would be far more expensive).

**Neutral:** PDPL compliance is design-ahead only until the first KSA user (recorded per-item in the DPA inventory); İYS/ETK and analytics consents are future obligations pinned to the features that trigger them; VERBIS exemption holds at solo-founder scale and is monitored, not actioned.
