## Context

`mvp.md` item 12 — *"Legal: privacy policy TR/AR/EN, KVKK/PDPL self-serve delete + export, terms, AI disclaimer"* — is the last MVP unit with no founder-blocked half. The delete + export halves shipped in M6.2 (ADR-019) and the in-app AI disclaimer shipped in M5.2 (ADR-017 D4). What remains, and what `architecture.md` §8 records honestly as **unbuilt**, is: consent screens per locale, the privacy policy and terms texts, and the DPA inventory. M6.3 additionally left the store's privacy/support URL fields deliberately EMPTY behind the `--allow-empty-urls` lint ratchet (ADR-020) — the policy text this ADR produces is the missing content for exactly that URL, though hosting stays founder-gated (operator item 8(c)).

This ADR was designed against three evidence briefs researched 2026-07-13 (KVKK incl. the 2024 Law-7499 amendments and the July-2024 transfer regulation; Saudi PDPL incl. the Sept-2024 full-enforcement posture and the still-unenacted 2025 draft amendments; Apple App Review Guidelines rev. June 2026). The briefs are evidence, **not legal advice**; where a reading is load-bearing and genuinely ambiguous, this ADR records both readings, implements the conservative one, and flags the founder's-lawyer question in `operator-expected.md` (the resume-prompt stopping condition — we do not play counsel).

**The shipped reality the legal surface must describe** (over-claiming is the failure mode — S020's guarantee-vs-mechanism class applied to legal text):

- Data collected, **located honestly** *(correction, rev 2, finding `honesty-1`: the rev-1 "all in Firestore eur3" claim over-located three categories)*: relationship content — free-text solo reflections and couple answers (≤2000 chars), profile (`status`, `contentLanguage`, `register`), couple metadata (pairing, timezone, streak), coach usage **counters** (never coach text), subscription entitlement mirror, invite records — lives in **Firestore eur3 (EU multi-region)**; **auth identifiers** (name/email/photo from Apple/Google, phone number for OTP) live in the **Firebase Auth backend, which is not region-pinned by the Firestore location**; **crash diagnostics** (Crashlytics, prod-only, content-free by sentinel-pinned rule: device/OS, stack traces, installation ID) are processed on **Google's Crashlytics backend outside eur3**; App Check attestation likewise is not eur3-resident. The consent-screen and policy copy must carry this split — "your relationship content is stored in the EU; some service data (sign-in identifiers, crash reports) is processed by Google outside the EU" — never an unqualified "all your data is in the EU."
- The coach is **ephemeral**: zero conversation content persisted server- or app-side (ADR-016/017); crisis detection runs transiently and no crisis flag is ever stored against a user (ADR-016 log discipline: crisis-path lines drop even `coupleId`).
- **No analytics SDK exists** (no Mixpanel, no Firebase Analytics — mvp item 11 is unbuilt). **No marketing/push delivery exists** (the APNs device half is operator item 4; `fcmTokens` is never written).
- Export (own data only, in-app, no email) and hard delete (whole-thread terminal, no push to the partner, billing survives) shipped in M6.2 with their honest bounds already in app copy.

### The legal-basis map (the analytical core)

| Processing | Basis (KVKK) | Basis (PDPL, at GCC phase) | Consent needed? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Auth identifiers, profile, pairing, streaks, entitlements — the service the user signs up for | Contract necessity (Art 5/2-c) | Contract/agreement implementation | **No** — and wrapping it in consent would be the KVKK "misleading consent" error (Icerik/5412) |
| Free-text reflections + couple answers + AI-coach messages + transient crisis detection | **Special-category, conservative reading** → explicit consent (Art 6, as amended 2024) | Sensitive data (health-inference via crisis detection; belief content in free text) → explicit consent, legitimate-interest unavailable | **Yes — exactly one explicit, separate, unbundled consent** |
| Cross-border transfer (EU hosting) | Contract necessity + **SCC filed with the Kurum** (Art 9) — **never consent** (the arızi/consent route is unavailable for continuous hosting) | SDAIA SCCs + TRA (GCC phase) | **No — notice only** |
| Analytics / profiling | açık rıza required (KVKK cookie/analytics decisions) | consent | Not collected — **feature does not exist**; consent arrives with the feature |
| Marketing / promotional push (İYS/ETK) | opt-in via İYS | consent | Not collected — **no push delivery exists**; recorded as a forward obligation on item 4's APNs slice |

**Load-bearing ambiguity 1 — special-category classification.** *Permissive reading:* relationship reflections are user-authored ordinary content; a keyword crisis-detector is a safety feature, not health processing. *Conservative reading (implemented):* free-text intimacy Q&A predictably contains sexual-life/health content, and the crisis detector derives a mental-health signal from text — together special-category processing under KVKK Art 6 / PDPL sensitive data, for which the only realistic basis is explicit consent. → **Lawyer question A** (recorded in operator-expected). *(Rev 2, finding `legal-2`: taking this classification also triggers the Kurul's adequate-measures decision for special-category data — recorded as its own operator/lawyer item in Decision 7.)*

**Load-bearing ambiguity 2 — consent as a condition of service.** KVKK consent must be freely given and cannot be extorted as a service condition (Icerik/5412); PDPL's anti-bundling rule carves out processing "specifically related" to the service. *Permissive reading:* the reflective content IS the product, so requiring this consent to use the reflective features is not conditioning — it is the PDPL carve-out and the KVKK rule's intended scope (the vice is tying *unrelated* processing). *Conservative reading (implemented):* same gate, but the consent copy states plainly WHY it is required (the processing is the service itself), declining leaves sign-out, **data export, and account deletion** available directly from the gate *(rev 2, blocking finding `appflow-1`)*, and nothing else (no analytics, no marketing) rides the same consent. → **Lawyer question B**.

**Load-bearing ambiguity 3 — what consent withdrawal must do to the already-stored corpus** *(added rev 2, findings `legal-1`/`honesty-2`)*. The free-text corpus rests on the withdrawn consent alone (no fallback basis exists for special-category data). *Erasure reading:* storage is processing ("işleme" includes "saklama"); withdrawing the sole basis compels erasure/quarantine of the stored corpus — withdrawal and erasure are not severable. *Prospective reading:* withdrawal operates ex nunc — new collection stops, and erasure remains a distinct right the subject exercises. **Implemented: the prospective reading, deliberately, on the repo's own DV doctrine** — ADR-018/019 are binding that a low-friction action must never destroy data ("failed attempts never destroy data — a wipe-after-N hands a destruction button to exactly the wrong person"): an auto-erasing withdraw behind one confirm dialog would let a partner with momentary phone access destroy the user's reflections, while the deliberate double-confirm + PIN deletion path is exactly the accident/coercion guard. The gate and the withdraw dialog expose "Delete account & data" directly, so erasure is one deliberate step away, never hidden. The withdrawal copy must say plainly that stored reflections **remain stored until deleted** — it must never imply the storage stopped. → **Lawyer question C**: does KVKK/PDPL withdrawal of the sole special-category basis compel controller-initiated erasure, or does the prospective reading + an adjacent self-serve erasure path suffice?

## Decision 1 — Exactly one consent, not a toggle farm; the notice is not a consent

The consent surface collects **one** explicit consent: processing of the user's relationship reflections, shared answers, and coach messages (the special-category-conservative bundle). There are **no per-purpose toggles** in v1 because no optional processing exists to toggle — analytics and marketing consents ship *with those features*, as their own unbundled opt-ins, when the features are built. The KVKK aydınlatma (information notice) is presented as a **notice, never a checkbox** — the Tebliğ requires notice and consent to be separate operations, and the KVKK's own guidance says consenting to what a contract basis already covers *misleads the data subject*.

**Rejected: a first-run consent wall covering everything.** Bundled "I accept everything" consent is legally invalid (battaniye rıza) and would poison the one consent we actually need.
**Rejected: per-purpose toggle farm now.** Toggles for processing that does not exist is consent theatre; every toggle would be a false claim about a live capability (the S020 class).
**Rejected: no consent at all (pure contract basis).** Defensible under the permissive reading, but it bets the launch on the aggressive side of both ambiguities; the Kurul is most aggressive exactly on special-category mishandling.

**Forward re-consent trigger (rev 2, finding `legal-4`):** selecting the live LLM provider (operator item 6 / M5.3) introduces a new special-category **recipient** and a new **cross-border leg** — a material change under the KVKK re-consent rule. **M5.3 must bump `CURRENT_LEGAL_VERSION` and re-gate**, with the provider named in the revised notice; recorded here so the M5.3 session inherits it as binding, not as a discovery.

## Decision 2 — Notice placement: the legal footer rides `ProviderActions` itself; documents render in-app

The aydınlatma must precede collection, and collection starts at sign-in (auth identifiers). *(Rev 2, finding `legal-3` + its deeper form, and `appflow-4`: the rev-1 placement on `_SignedOutView` alone provably missed the cold-opened deep-link invitee, who signs in from `PartnerPreviewScreen`, and the sign-in `_ErrorView` retry surface.)* The **legal footer — one short line plus two text links (Privacy Notice, Terms) opening the in-app document screens — is co-located with the `ProviderActions` widget itself**, the actual auth-identifier collection affordance, so every present and future surface that renders the sign-in buttons (`_SignedOutView`, `_ErrorView`, `PartnerPreviewScreen`'s join card) carries the notice **by construction**, not by per-screen patching. No checkbox, no acknowledgement, no blocking step pre-auth: the duty is availability-before-collection, and a pre-auth ack would have nowhere durable to live anyway.

Apple 5.1.1(i) requires the privacy policy *"within the app in an easily accessible manner"* — the in-app documents satisfy this **without waiting for the founder's domain** (no hosted URL exists; the store URL fields stay empty behind the ADR-020 ratchet, unchanged this session).

**Rejected: notice-as-first-run-interstitial (blocking splash).** Heavier than the duty requires, adds a pre-auth screen with no durable state, and pushes users through a legal wall before they can even see what the app is.

## Decision 3 — The consent gate: a per-uid `OnboardingGate` branch, after profile capture, before every home

A new gate-child branch in `OnboardingGate`, evaluated **immediately after the `value == null → ProfileCaptureScreen` branch and before every other branch** (paired home, coupleEnded notice, pending invite, solo home):

```
value != null && !hasCurrentConsent(value)  →  ConsentGateScreen(uid)
```

where `hasCurrentConsent` = `profile.consent != null && profile.consent.version >= currentLegalVersion`. Gate children are rendered, never pushed (uncloseable by back — the `CoupleEndedNoticeScreen` mold). `PrivacyGuard` already sits above it.

- **After profile capture, deliberately.** Profile fields (`status`, `contentLanguage`, `register`) are ordinary data on the contract basis and are needed to render the gate in the right language and register; the special-category consent gates the *reflective content*, which begins at the homes. This also means the `recordConsent` callable can use the fail-closed `.update()` mold against an existing `users/{uid}` doc (Decision 4). *Rejected: gate before profile capture* — it would force a create-or-merge write path (weaker than `.update()`'s native profile-missing failure) and would gate ordinary-basis collection behind a consent that isn't about it.
- **Before the `coupleEnded` notice, deliberately:** consent is a precondition on the reflective surfaces; notices are content. B returning after an app update sees consent once, then the notice.
- **The screen:** summary paragraphs (what is processed, why, the honest data-location split from Context, the processors, the rights, the withdrawal path), links to both full documents (opening the **document screens directly** — Decision 5), an 18+ **eligibility statement** framed as a Terms condition, deliberately not part of the consent sentence *(rev 2, finding `legal-5`: the attestation is an eligibility declaration, not a processing consent — co-located but severed in wording)*, **one affirmative `FilledButton`** ("I consent and continue" — a deliberate action, never a pre-ticked box), and — *rev 2, blocking finding `appflow-1`* — three escape affordances: **sign out**, **Download my data** (pushes the existing `ExportScreen`), and **Delete account & data** (pushes the existing `DeleteAccountScreen`), so a decliner or a withdrawn user is never trapped with fewer rights than a consented one, and Apple 5.1.1(v)'s "easy to find" deletion holds even for a reviewer who declines the gate. Busy/error handling follows the settings-row pattern (busy flag, error resolver, honest failure copy, retry).
- **No optimistic local grant:** the gate flips only when the server-confirmed field arrives on the already-streaming `profileStreamProvider` — offline acceptance shows the error line and retries; a device-local "consented" that the server never saw would be an unprovable consent. **Stale-after-accept state (rev 2, finding `appflow-3`):** if the callable returns success but the streamed `consent.version` still fails `hasCurrentConsent` (an app binary ahead of the deployed server constant), the gate shows a persistent honest error line ("could not be completed — try again later") instead of silently re-offering the CTA loop; the sentinel + deploy-ordering rule below make this state unreachable in practice, but the UI never spins on it.
- **Version bump ⇒ re-gate:** `currentLegalVersion` is a Dart const; a future material policy revision bumps it (with the server constant and the document front-matter, same diff, pinned by the three-way sentinel in Decision 4) and every user re-consents on next open.
- **Migration story (recorded, not silent):** existing sessions — the founder couple; nothing is deployed to any real device today — have no `consent` field; on first open after this ships they see the consent screen **once**, one tap, then their session continues exactly as before. Nothing else about their state is touched.
- **Bootstrap untouched:** the gate reads the streamed profile; no new pre-frame await, no `entrypoint_sentinel_test` edit, no new `LocalFlagStore` key.

## Decision 4 — Consent state is server-owned: `users/{uid}.consent` via a `recordConsent` callable; the server stamps the version

`users/{uid}.consent = { version: <int>, acceptedAt: <server Timestamp>, ageAttested: true }`, written **only** by a new `recordConsent` callable (admin SDK), following the `updateNotificationPrivacy` mold: pure validator in `data-rights-core.ts`, `.update()` service (missing profile fails natively → `failed-precondition {reason:'profile-missing'}`), onCall shell with the static-error discipline and `logDataRightsEvent` (op/outcome/latency only — never uid).

- **The server stamps its own `CURRENT_LEGAL_VERSION` constant; the client sends no version** *(rev 2, findings `appflow-3`/`testability-1` — this replaces rev 1's client-claimed version + known-set validation)*. The wire is `{withdraw: boolean}` — `withdraw:false` grants (writes `{version: CURRENT_LEGAL_VERSION, acceptedAt: serverTimestamp(), ageAttested: true}`), `withdraw:true` clears (`FieldValue.delete()`, the `notificationPrivacy` clear mold). No client-claimed version means no junk-version surface and no client/server mismatch rejection — the residual skew (app const ahead of a not-yet-deployed server const) surfaces as the gate's stale-after-accept state, never a brick loop.
- **`ageAttested` is recorded and exported** *(rev 2, finding `honesty-3`)*: the Terms lean on the 18+ posture, so the attestation the gate collects must live in the provable record and the subject's export, not in unversioned screen copy — the exact proves-nothing-flag failure Decision 4 exists to avoid. Honest bound: the flag attests that the grant flowed through the gate that displays the eligibility statement; a modified client calling the callable directly self-attests — the same client-honesty bound as Decision 4a below, recorded.
- **Three version sources, pinned by a source-sentinel test** *(rev 2, finding `testability-1`; replaces rev 1's "coordinated by procedure")*: an app-side sentinel test reads all three trees from the `app/` test cwd (the `device_privacy_channel_parity_test` cross-tree precedent) and asserts `app currentLegalVersion == functions CURRENT_LEGAL_VERSION == docs/legal/README.md version:` — a partial bump fails **red in CI**, in both directions (the app-ahead brick AND the silent under-gate where documents change but no re-consent fires). The bump procedure in `docs/legal/README.md` additionally records the deploy-ordering rule: **the Functions constant deploys before (or with) the app binary that raises the gate's expectation** — moot today (nothing is deployed anywhere; the first deploy ships rules, functions, and binary together) but binding for the deploy era.
- **Why server-side, not the ADR-017 device-local precedent:** KVKK puts the proof burden (ispat yükü) on the controller — consent must be demonstrable with version + timestamp, must survive reinstall, and is itself personal data (export + erasure). A `LocalFlagStore` flag proves nothing, dies with prefs, and is invisible to export. The coach ack stays device-local because it is a UX acknowledgement, not a legal consent — the distinction is now written down.
- **The M6.2 rules invariant applies in full** (ADR-019 D6: *every server-owned users field needs an update freeze AND a create forbid*): the users create rule gains `!('consent' in request.resource.data)`, the update rule gains `request.resource.data.get('consent', null) == resource.data.get('consent', null)`, and both clauses get the mutant treatment (create-forbid mutant, freeze mutant, positive control, delete-deny already covered).
- **A field, deliberately not a subcollection.** `users/{uid}/consents/*` would need a new cascade sweep step (the M6.2 own-subtree sweep enumerates only `soloAnswers` — an unswept subcollection is orphaned residue), a new rules block against the deny-all catch-all, and an append-only history the data-minimization posture doesn't want. The field rides the existing cascade's `deleteRef(selfRef)` untouched. **Honest cost:** only the *latest* acceptance is retained — re-consent to v2 overwrites the v1 record. The proof burden concerns the basis of *current* processing, which the latest record carries; recorded as the accepted trade-off.
- **Withdrawal — the prospective reading (Load-bearing ambiguity 3), honest and reachable:** the withdraw affordance lives on the **Settings-reached legal screen only** (Decision 5), behind one confirm dialog whose copy states plainly: withdrawal stops the reflective features (the gate returns); **your stored reflections remain stored until you delete them** — with "Delete account & data" offered right there and on the gate itself. The copy never implies the storage-processing stopped *(rev 2, findings `legal-1`/`honesty-2`)*. The path is in-app, free, one confirm, no re-verification — the substantive "as easy as giving" standard (no fee, no medium shift, no barriers), not tap-count parity.
- **Export lane:** `projectProfile` gains `consent: {version, acceptedAtMs, ageAttested}` **iff set** — the same iff-set optional-field *code pattern* as `notificationPrivacy` (which, for the record, shipped as part of the original v1 wire and is NOT a precedent for unbumped additions). **`FORMAT_VERSION` bumps 1 → 2** *(rev 2, finding `security-2`: ADR-019's frozen-wire invariant reads "a shape change bumps this," unqualified; the bump is costless on a one-way parserless download and keeps the invariant intact)*.
- **Client parse is junk-safe, and the guard is test-pinned** *(rev 2, finding `testability-2`)*: the app `Profile` model parses `consent` tolerantly — non-map, missing keys, non-int version, missing `acceptedAt` ⇒ treated as absent ⇒ gate shows (fail closed) — pinned by a malformed-shape unit-test matrix, and both this guard and the `version >=` boundary are mutation-checked (a junk shape coerced to "present" is the gate-bypass inversion; `_GateErrorView` only catches throws, so silent coercion needs its own net).

## Decision 4a — The rules carry a consent predicate on the special-category writes; the honest enforcement bound is recorded *(added rev 2, blocking finding `security-1`)*

Rev 1 called consent "a processing precondition" while the only mechanism was client-side navigation — the S020 guarantee-vs-mechanism class. Rev 2 both adds the cheap server-side mechanism and records the bound the mechanism cannot carry:

- **Mechanism:** the `soloAnswers` create/update rules and the couple-answers create/update rules gain `&& get(/databases/$(database)/documents/users/$(request.auth.uid)).data.get('consent', null) != null` — one extra rules `get()` per answer write, the established `exists(users/…)` write-guard pattern from ADR-019. Each new clause gets a **mutant** (weaken it → a consentless write must succeed) plus a positive control. Migration-safe: the gate records consent before any home renders, and nothing is deployed anywhere today (the first deploy ships rules + gate together).
- **Honest bound (recorded, not silent):** the predicate is **presence-only, version-blind** — rules cannot read the Dart/TS version constants, so after a future version bump a modified client holding a stale-version consent passes the rules while the app re-gates. The rules predicate closes the "special-category content stored with **zero** consent ever recorded" hole; it does not make the rules layer equivalent to the gate. Full parity is not claimed.
- **`coachProxy` deliberately unchanged:** its wire contract is FROZEN (ADR-016), it stores no conversation content, and the deployed default provider fails closed — a pre-consent call by a modified client processes nothing durable and reaches no live LLM today. The consent precondition for the live coach lands with M5.3, which this ADR already binds to a version bump + re-gate (Decision 1) and its own ADR + reviews.
- **`exportData` and `deleteAccount` deliberately carry NO consent precondition:** the data-subject rights must be exercisable exactly when consent is absent or withdrawn — a consent-gated rights surface would invert the design (new fail-direction row).

## Decision 5 — In-app legal documents: authored in `docs/legal/`, byte-synced to bundled assets, rendered without new dependencies

- **Authoring home:** `docs/legal/privacy-policy.{tr,ar,en}.md` and `docs/legal/terms.{tr,ar,en}.md` (+ `docs/legal/README.md` carrying the version, the review-PENDING flags, and the bump procedure incl. the deploy-ordering rule). The TR privacy policy is structured to double as the **aydınlatma metni** — it carries the Art-10 minimums (controller identity, purposes, recipient groups, collection method **and legal basis stated explicitly per purpose**, Art-11 rights) and the Apple 5.1.1(i) minimums (data/uses, third parties held to equal protection, retention/deletion, consent revocation) and the PDPL Art-12 content (design-ahead). One document per locale serving all three masters — a solo-founder app does not need parallel instruments.
- **Bundle sync:** the six documents are byte-copied to `app/assets/legal/` (new pubspec asset dir). Drift is pinned by an app test that reads both trees and asserts byte-equality (`../docs/legal/...` is reachable from `flutter test`'s `app/` cwd — the shipped-packs-off-disk precedent). The content-pack `--sync` tool precedent is deliberately NOT extended — six static files don't need a tool, they need a red CI on drift.
- **Rendering:** a minimal deterministic renderer (~40 lines): `#`/`##` lines → title/section styles, `- ` lines → bulleted rows, blank-line-separated paragraphs → body `Text`. Total function — any unrecognized line renders as body text; it can never throw. Inside the established `SingleChildScrollView → Column(Text…)` mold. **Rejected: `flutter_markdown`/`url_launcher`/WebView** — each would be the app's first such dependency, for six static documents; and a URL launcher has no URL to launch until the founder hosts one.
- **Test seam (rev 2, finding `testability-3`):** the document screen loads its body through an **injected `AssetBundle`** — widget tests and goldens override it with the `StaticAssetBundle` pattern via a `shippedLegalBundle()` off-disk reader (the `shippedSoloPackBundle` precedent), because real `rootBundle` loads never complete inside the widget-test fake-async zone and would wedge `pumpAndSettle`. Named here so the implementer builds the seam, not discovers the wedge.
- **Two surfaces, one discriminator (rev 2, finding `appflow-2` + the D4/D5 rev-1 self-contradiction):** `LegalDocumentScreen` renders one document in the resolved locale (EN fallback) — this is what the sign-in footer, the consent gate's links, and the **paywall links row** (Terms + Privacy, Apple 3.1.2/Schedule-2) open **directly; documents only, no consent controls anywhere on these paths**. `LegalScreen` — the hub with the two document tiles **plus consent status ("Consented on <date>, version N") and the Withdraw action** — exists **only behind the Settings tile** (between Export and Delete, the data-rights cluster). The discriminator is *reached-from-Settings*, never *signed-in*: a pre-consent gate must never show "Withdraw," and a subscription surface must never carry a consent control in front of App Review.
- **ARB:** new `legal*` and `consent*` prefixes ×3 locales for all chrome and consent copy, guarded by a new `legal_arb_guard_test.dart` (tri-locale parity + placeholder shape; the coach digit-run rule deliberately not cloned). Links are `TextButton`s (the S019 `FilledButton` min-width gotcha).

Founder/legal call, recorded not made.

## Decision 7 — `docs/dpa-inventory.md`: the processor register, cross-checked against §8
